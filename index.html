Girls Who Code

Teaching Assistant

Closing the gender gap in technology is something I am passionate about, and something I have spent a lot of time working on, carving out space for women in tech one step at a time. Girls Who Code is the non-profit organization that jumpstarted my tech career, and when deciding what I wanted to do this summer, I knew I had to give back. As a TA for the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program (partnered with Warner Brothers), I guided a class of twenty girls, teaching them the basics of computer science using languages and programs like Scratch, Python, Arduino C, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This work was meaningful for me because I get to guide the next wave of computer scientists. I'm proud to have had a hand in the girls' journey into tech. My time as a Girl Who Codes was three years ago, and spending time with these amazing students reminded me why I decided to study computer science in the first place. This is the summer I learned about sisterhood.

Carleton College Summer Computer Science Institute

Student

I applied to this program through Questbridge. I answered the prompts and gave them my resume, never thinking that anything would come of it. As a first-generation Mexican-American, I didn't think that programs like this were for people like me. Spending three weeks by myself in a different state when I had never even left California (except to drive to Mexico) required a huge leap of faith for me and my family. When I received the phone call that I would be flown across the country to Northfield, Minnesota, and be a part of a three-week program on a full scholarship, I was in shock. After taking a few minutes to recover, I told my parents and got ready to get to work. There, I learned the basics of programming, robotics, and human-computer interaction. If Girls Who Code got me started on programming, then this was the summer I learned about what it meant to be a computer scientist, to want to go above and beyond for something that means a lot.

July 2017 - August 2017

Girls Who Code

Student

Girls Who Code fell in my lap through serendipity. I remember that I got a random email as a high school sophomore, and the subject line was, "Do you want to stand out in college applications?" Being a forward-thinking go-getter, I said, "Of course!" And I am very, very glad I did. At the time, I more or less knew that I wanted to study computer science, but I honesly wasn't sure what it was. This was a big summer of firsts for me. My parents worked and couldn't drive me to and from the program, so I learned to take the Metro for the first time, had an hour-long commute for the first time, and wrote my first "hello world" program. From there, everything fell into place. I was introduced to programming concepts I didn't even know were possible. My final project was a website dedicated to spreading awareness of and giving support to survivors of college campus sexual assault, inspired by the documentary The Hunting Ground (on Netflix!). This was the summer I learned about what it really takes to be a girl who codes.
